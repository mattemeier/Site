Research Interests

My research seeks to understand normal individual differences in working memory capacity.Working memory capacity is the functional limit of how many memory representations can be held in an activated state simultaneously in the face of interference and shifts of conscious focus in the service of ongoing cognitive activities. People who recall more items on these tasks (i.e., have a higher working memory capacity) tend to outperform lower scoring subjects on an impressive array of complex cognitive tasks (e.g., reading comprehension, multi-tasking, reasoning) suggesting that working memory capacity is a general and important cognitive construct. My research aims to explain why.
